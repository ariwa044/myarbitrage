What is arbitrage?

Arbitrage is the practice of exploiting price differences of identical assets across different markets. When an asset trades at different prices simultaneously, we buy at the lower price and sell at the higher price, capturing the difference as profit.

Why it works every day

Markets are constantly creating inefficiencies due to timing delays, geographic differences, and information asymmetry. These micro-opportunities appear thousands of times daily. While individual trades are small, the volume and consistency generate substantial returns.

Risk profile

Unlike directional bets, arbitrage profits come from market inefficiencies, not price movements. Returns are largely independent of whether markets rise or fall.

Historical performance

Arbitrage strategies have been used by institutional traders for decades. We've democratized this approach for individual investors.
